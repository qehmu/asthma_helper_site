Inhaler and Peak Flow Meter

Step-by-step instructions for the correct use of the pocket inhaler and peak flow meter for accurate measurements.

Checklists that help

A simple lifestyle with asthma begins with clear actions. Use checklists every day and when preparing for treatment.

  • Check the inhaler and spare cartridges.
  • Write down the frequency of breathing and the level of difficulty.
  • Measure peak flow in a calm state.
  • Evaluate factors that may have caused an exacerbation.

How to use a pocket inhaler

  1. Shake the inhaler and remove the cap.
  2. Take a deep breath, then place the mouthpiece in your mouth.
  3. Press and inhale slowly at the same time.
  4. Hold your breath for 5–10 seconds, then exhale.
  5. Rinse the mouthpiece and store the device dry.

How to use a peak flow meter

Regular measurements help you notice deterioration before significant coughing or breathing difficulty.

  1. Stand up straight and exhale as deeply as possible.
  2. Strongly exhale into the device, holding it steady.
  3. Write down the result and repeat three times, choosing the best reading.
  4. Compare the reading with your usual value.
🌬️

Slow Breathing

Inhale slowly through your nose, hold your breath and exhale gently through your mouth. Repeat 4–6 times.

🧘

Box Breathing

Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4, exhale for 4, pause for 4. Helps reduce anxiety and restore breathing rhythm.

💗

Body Relaxation

Start by relaxing your shoulders and neck, then focus on steady, calm breathing.

Self-Help Guidelines for Exacerbations

If an attack or suffocation begins, follow a clear action plan.

  • Sit up straight, do not lie down.
  • Use your rescue inhaler according to your plan.
  • Breathe slowly and calmly, repeating the technique.
  • If your condition does not improve — seek medical help.
